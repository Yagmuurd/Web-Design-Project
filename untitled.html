Future of Electric Transformers & Urban Planning

The adverse effects of electrical transformers in settlements on human life are increasing day by day. In order to prevent the high electromagnetic radiation that people are exposed to at any time, new ways of carrying electricity are being researched. This conference will enlighten you about the future of electrical transformers and city planning through interacting with the experts.

March 1- March 2 , 2022, (Tuesday and Wednesday). From" 11:45 to 16:00 ", CerModern.

SPEAKERS

Emma Hills

Electrical Engineer

Hills, currently an instructor at the University of London, is interested in developing mechanisms and tools to design large-scale and productive systems emphasizing environmental and efficient monitoring. She also works on the subject of electric electronics and manipulation technologies to find new ways of transforming energy.

Adam Wills

Urban Planner

Wills is an urban planner with 20 years of experience. Excels at creating and practicing intuitive and efficient tools which have become the basis of new projects that make municipalities plan more efficint and functional cities. He now works on engaging the energy sector with urban planning in his enterprise company established in 2019.

Sakura Mika

Industrial Designer

Mika spent ten years designing products, specializing on energy industry. Her talent made her led three environmental-friendly based design projects, and one of them was awarded A'Design Award and Competition in 2021. Now she works on a project for engaging the electricity industry with urban planning by including her innovative knowledge.

Mike Jabbar

Medical Doctor

Jabbar is a medical doctor in Dubai who has achieved 150 million worth of prize for his project on finding solutions for the adverse affects of urbanization. In his 30's, Mike was promoted to Nobel Prize for his success on this field of study. He now manages to address direct effects of electrical transformators' settled on residential areas on human body.
